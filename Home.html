ABOUT SBMA

The Subic Bay Metropolitan Authority (SBMA) is a government-owned corporation tasked with the administration, development, and management of the
Subic Bay Freeport Zone in the Philippines. Established in 1992 under Republic Act 7227, the SBMA is responsible for transforming the former U.S.
naval base into a thriving industrial, commercial, and tourism hub.
With a mandate to promote economic growth, generate employment opportunities, and attract investments, the SBMA plays a vital role in driving the development
of the Subic Bay Freeport Zone. Through its various programs and initiatives, the SBMA has been successful in establishing a business-friendly environment that
has attracted both local and international investors.
The SBMA operates under a comprehensive regulatory framework that ensures the proper management and utilization of resources within the Freeport Zone. This includes
overseeing lease agreements, enforcing environmental regulations, and providing infrastructure and utilities to support businesses operating within the zone.
In addition to its economic development efforts, the SBMA is committed to promoting sustainable growth and environmental conservation in the Subic Bay area. The
authority works closely with various stakeholders, including local communities, business owners, and government agencies, to ensure that development activities
are conducted in a responsible and sustainable manner.
Overall, the SBMA serves as a key driver of economic progress and development in the Subic Bay region. With its strong commitment to fostering growth, promoting
investment, and ensuring sustainability, the authority continues to play a crucial role in shaping the future of the Subic Bay Freeport Zone.
